Chapter Summaries

  • The Hearth and the Salamander
  • The novel begins with the protagonist, Guy Montag, going through an average night of work
  • It is in the 24th century and much of America has changed dramatically
  • Montag's work consists of finding people in possession of literature, which has been banned, and burning their books and homes, and ironically enough he is called a fireman
  • Montag is very pleased with his work and doesn't find any flaws in it
  • Following work one evening Montag walks home and runs into his neighbor Clarisse McClellan
  • Clarisse is a young and interesting girl, she doesn't exhibit the normal attitude of teenagers during her time as she is very interested in nature, and she despises television and fast cars
  • Montag is slightly changed by this brief encounter as he begins to reflect on the world he lives in
  • Before their conversation is over Clarisse asks Montag one final question, is he happy
  • After some thought and speculation Montag realizes that he is anything but
  • When Montag returns home he is surprised to find his wife Mildred unconscious and overdosed on sleeping pills
  • Frantic, Montag calls the paramedics who come and save her life, the paramedics also remark that situations like this occur quite often
  • This event sparks more questioning within Montag's mind as he begins to see his society as very impersonal and tragic
  • The following day Mildred continues life completely normal with seemingly no recollection of the previous night
  • Montag heads out for work and on his way has another encounter with Clarisse which she begins to question his happiness and feelings about life again
  • Montag eventually reaches work where he is immediately growled at by the mechanical hound that the firemen use
  • The hound has the ability to hunt down a specific target and quickly and effortlessly annihilate it
  • Montag is annoyed with the savage mechanical beast and addresses his commander Chief Beatty about his concerns, yet his is ignored
  • One night at work the firemen are called to a house where a woman is suspected of owning books
  • Out of defiance to the firemen, the woman sets her house ablaze and perishes inside with all the burned books
  • Montag takes the opportunity to steal a book from the house and he decides to take it home even though it is illegal
  • Montag returns home and lies in a sleepless state in his bed contemplating his loveless and pathetic relationship
  • He remembers that he has not seen Clarisse in a few days and asks his wife whether or not she has seen her
  • Mildred informs him that Clarisse was killed four days earlier by a speeding car
  • Montag is very upset by the news and appalled that Mildred had failed to mention this earlier
  • The next day the fire chief, Chief Beatty, arrives at the Montag house where he lectures Montag on the importance of living a very structured and homogenous life
  • During the meeting with Beatty, Mildred notices the outline of a book under the pillow however she decides not to turn her husband in
  • Following Beatty's visit, Mildred questions what would happen if he were caught with a book, she learns that Montag would have 24 hours to come to his senses and destroy it or else face punishment from the other firemen
  • Montag begins to become increasingly paranoid about Beatty, and he takes the chance to confess to Mildred that he owns over twenty books
  • Millie is shocked and demands he dispose of them, however as she rants about the dangers of books Montag begins to read
  • The Sieve and the Sand
  • Montag spends the rest of the rainy afternoon uneasily reading through books while Millie sits idly.
  • The rest of that day is spent reading by Montag while his wife sits lazily around the house
  • That afternoon Montag's reading is interrupted by scratching at the door by the mechanical hound
  • The hound has come looking for a reason to cause trouble at the Montag house however leaves without any issue
  • Mildred is very worried that they will get caught, however Montag puts her in his place saying that what does it matter, all over the world people are at war and are suffering and their community is centered around leisure and a lack of competence
  • Montag thinks back to a meeting he had with a man named Faber years back
  • Faber had been an English professor decades ago and during their encounter it was obvious to Montag that Faber was possessing a book, yet Montag did nothing
  • Montag deliberates with himself what book he will hand over to Captain Beatty in order to avoid trouble and he wonders whether Beatty knows of a specific book he possesses
  • He decides that he will seek out Faber to have a copy of the Bible made and then turn that in to Beatty
  • On the way to Faber's home Montag takes the subway and thinks to himself about how numb the world had become, he wonders whether he will ever find a real purpose in life
  • At Faber's home Faber is at first hesitant to see Montag as he fears it may be a trap however he eventually lets him in
  • Faber remarks about how upset he is with himself for never standing up for books when they were initially banned
  • Faber tells Montag that books have been banned to more effectively control society as books had increasingly made people more uncomfortable by providing their minds with speculations
  • The men discuss ways to return books to society and at first talk about planting books in the homes of firemen around the country in hopes it will spark controversy and a revival of literature
  • Faber backs off on that idea as he believes that they should wait till war overruns the society and then start anew
  • Montag asks Faber with guidance so that he will not be caught for possessing books
  • The two agree that they will have two way radios so Faber can monitor Montag's situations and tell him how to act
  • Montag returns to his house to find Mildred meeting with some other women as they watch television
  • Montag is disturbed by their seemingly lack of caring
  • He begins to engage them with conversation on the war and life
  • He learns however that in fact they have no compassion for what should be their loved ones and really only care about themselves and amusement
  • Montag also learns that they even voted for the last president based on looks
  • Montag further causes a commotion in the house by bringing out a poetry book and reading a poem to the women
  • Mrs. Phelps, one of the women, is left in tears after listening to the poem while the other women, Mrs. Bowles, is very angry that Montag would put them in that situation
  • Following this Montag prepares to leave for work after stopping to check on his books
  • When he checks his hiding place he notices some are missing and that Mildred must have destroyed some
  • Montag is frustrated but must go to work so he asks into the radio how he should act in the fire station
  • Montag enters the station and calmly hands over a book to Beatty to be destroyed before playing cards with the other men
  • Beatty gives Montag a hard time about literature and its uselessness
  • While they are playing cards an alarm interrupts them and they respond to the call
  • As they arrive at the house where the call comes from Montag realizes it is his own home
  • An alarm comes in, and they go to answer it.
  • When they arrive at the location, Montag looks up to discover they have been called to his own home.
  • Burning Bright
  • Montag is numb with disbelief.
  • Montag is stunned to have been discovered and Beatty is very critical of him for not turning the books in
  • Faber suggests to Montag to run away over the radio
  • Montag can not escape however due to the hound and Beatty demands he burn down his own house room by room
  • After Montag burns down his house with a flamethrower Beatty strikes him and knocks the two-way radio out of his ear
  • Beatty realizes what it is and states that he will find the other person and pay them a visit
  • Montag builds up his courage and sets Beatty ablaze with the flamethrower, then he destroys the mechanical hound but not before being stabbed in the leg with a poison needle
  • Montag then makes his escape into the night but not before grabbing the remaining books he had left hidden in his garden
  • Montag makes his way towards Faber's home but not before he stops to plant some books in a fellow fireman's home
  • Once at Faber's he is told that he should continue on and follow the river to the train tracks and from there search for a hobo "walking camp"
  • Before leaving they take the chance to check the television and learn that Montag is being hunted by another mechanical hound
  • Montag heads to the river where he douses himself in alcohol and then changes clothing before hoping in the water and floating down the river
  • Montag eventually reaches the train tracks and begins looking for people that he can mix in with
  • He has a strange feeling that perhaps Clarisse had once been down this path
  • Montag eventually finds a group of men looking rather gruff and scruffy gathered around a fire
  • A man by the name of Granger addresses Montag and asks him to join the group and have some coffee
  • The group then makes Montag a rather bitter tasting drink that will change his chemical balance and help hide him from the mechanical hound
  • The group introduces themselves to Montag and he learns that they are all intellectuals
  • They continue traveling on and then rest for the night
  • During the night Montag sees bombs being dropped on the city and he realizes that Mildred is probably dead
  • He thinks to himself that she has lived a very pathetic and useless life
  • Granger compares society to a phoenix, every so often it destroys itself only to be rebuilt again
  • The novel ends with the men heading off in the direction of the city to start anew
